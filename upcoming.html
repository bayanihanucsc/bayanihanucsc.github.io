Noon At Ngayon

"Then and Now"

"Follow the story of Ramona Gonzaga, a proud Pilipinx, who returns home after a year at UCCP to face a political and social divide between her peers and family before she is murdered in cold blood. But who did it? Her parents, Maribel and Joseph Gonzaga think it was her boyfriend. But something isn't right. We follow the night of her death and find clues that will point us to the truth. The simplest answer isn't always the right one. And the truth we thought we knew might be the one lying to us from the start."

What is PCC?

PCC is an annual production that Bayanihan puts together to showcase out Filipino culture as well as address pertinent issues in society. This year, we are tackling the issues of colonial mentality and colorism. Our production features all of Bayanihans's performing arts troupes interwoven together to tell an original story surrounding Pilipino history and culture. Our annual production provides an open space and dialogue for our cast, faculty, and the rest of the community to come together and learn about another aspect of the Pilipinx/Pilipino history and culture.
